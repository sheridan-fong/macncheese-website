Macaroni and Cheese

a dish of pasta and cheese sauce, traditionally using macaroni and cheddar, baked in a casserole or sometimes cooked on a stovetop.


The Pioneer: Thomas Jefferson

The dish traces its roots back to Italy; however, Thomas Jefferson is credited by historians for bringing about and

popularizing the dish. He ate the dish in Italy and loved it so much that he brought a pasta maker back to the USA to recreate his Italian experience.

Fun Fact: Mac n cheese was first served in the whitehouse in 1802!

A Simple Mac n Cheese Recipe.

  • 8 ounces of box elbow macaroni
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
